## 2. What the D-3a carve-out should allow

**Permit:** a **server-side-internal** resolution `linked PC (masa) → masa.aktif_adisyon_no → the one active adisyon`, used **only** to fetch the minimized non-identity projection of that bill + its `detay` lines + the Adeks-reflected discount row — **triggered only by a valid, burned QR handshake token** that bound the app session to that PC (K-OS-009). The physical desk opt-in is the authorization; no client-supplied identifier is involved.

**Continue to forbid:** reading/deriving `adisyon.uye_no` or any member resolution (the chain terminates at the bill projection, never at identity); propagating `aktif_adisyon_no` or raw `adisyon_no` to the client or into Adeks domain models (server-internal only; the client never sees a raw bill id); any customer-supplied raw bill-number lookup (enumeration surface stays closed); following transfer/merge pointers `odeme_adisyon_no`/`diger_adisyon_tutar` (SR-003-12); historical-bill access on the guest path (SR-003-11).

Net: the carve-out permits the **reach** (PC → active bill) while preserving **every** D-3a guarantee (identity-free, non-propagating, no `uye_no`), scoped to the QR-linked session and the single active bill.

## 3. What must remain hard-excluded (unchanged, reinforced)

Non-negotiable, carried from §4.2 + advisor must-have #3: `adisyon.uye_no` / `kasaislem.uye_no` / `kuyruk.uye_no` (never read, even to "confirm" the guest) · entire `dbo.uye` / `dbo.basvuru` / `dbo.kullanici` · any `sifre` · `uye.bakiye` + `uyesinif` credit/balance · staff FKs (`kullanici_no`, `iptal_kullanici_no`) · member points/balance/history/profile · free-text likely to carry PII (`adisyon.aciklama`/`iptal_aciklama`; `kasaislem.aciklama` beyond the fixed code format) · `adisyon.uye_indirim`/`_oran`/`ek_indirim` (stub/retired) · `adisyon.kasaislem_no` (stub) · `masa.aktif_adisyon_no` as a propagated FK · transfer/merge targets + historical bills.

**Enforcement:** column-level deny grants on the RO login **at the DB grant/query layer, not only UI** (SR-003-6); the login physically cannot select the denied columns. Evidenced by SR-006 security-regression tests. **These stay load-bearing regardless of session-member retirement** (§7) — the columns still exist and legacy / long-running sessions still carry them.

## 7. Retirement-agnostic invariance

Baked into v1.2 so the design is safe whether Selcafe member sessions continue or are later retired:

- **Member-linkage exclusion is unconditional.** The controls (grant-level `uye_no`/member-table deny, no member resolution, no history) apply to **every** bill read. The read never keys off member presence. Sessions continue → excluded columns populated but never read (memberless projection by construction). Sessions retired → new bills have null `uye_no`; same controls apply unchanged.
- **No control may key off member presence/absence.** Explicitly foreclose the inference "members retired → relax column-deny." Mixed state (legacy + long-running old sessions carry `uye_no`) means the deny stays regardless.
- **Retirement is defense-in-depth, not a load-bearing assumption.** If it later lands it reduces residual risk but changes **no** control — the v1.2 design is identical with or without it. v1.2 can therefore be authored/merged/piloted now and retirement decided later; neither forces re-deciding the other.
- Transfer/merge (SR-003-12) and current-bill-only (SR-003-11) are session-scope controls — robust either way.

`[LOCKED PRINCIPLE CONFLICT]`: **none.** Read-only posture D-1 preserved (gated read, not a write; discount reflection is the human cashier bridge, K-OS-008). Append-only ledgers untouched. Member-identity exclusion preserved.
